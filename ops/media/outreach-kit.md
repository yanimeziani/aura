## 2. HACKER NEWS

**Title**: Show HN: Aura – Sovereign AI monorepo: Zig runtime, Android cockpit, 10-min recovery protocol

**Post body**:
```
I built Aura as a one-person sovereign AI stack. The core idea: you should be able to drop your phone in a river and be back to full operations on a fresh device in exactly 10 minutes.

The stack:
- Cerberus: Zig agent runtime, <1MB binary, 3230+ tests
- Pegasus: Android mobile cockpit (Kotlin/Compose) with biometric auth and WebAuthn passkeys
- Autonomous agents (Career-Twin, SDR) with human-in-the-loop gates and cost caps
- One-script VPS deployment on Debian

Why Zig? Performance, no hidden allocators, single binary with no dependencies. The entire runtime cross-compiles to x86_64 in seconds.

Why sovereignty? I've seen too many small operations locked into vendor stacks they don't control. Aura is the antithesis — you own every byte, every key, every deployment.

Prior work: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06212

Live launch docs: https://meziani.org/launch/

GitHub: https://github.com/yanimeziani/aura

Happy to answer questions about the architecture, the Zig runtime, or the sovereignty protocol.
```
